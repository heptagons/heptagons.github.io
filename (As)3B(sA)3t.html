(As)3B(sA)3t

(As)3B(sA)3t is the class of heptagons with
  1. Six equal angles A and a single angle B.
  2. Six equal sides s and a single side t.

(As)3B(sA)3t heptagon

Angles A,B

For such heptagons we have (\( 0 \le A \le 5\pi/6 \)) and (\( 0 \le B \le 5\pi\)): \begin{align*} 6A + B &= 5\pi \end{align*} At vertex P1 there is an angle called \( C \). At vertex P2 there is an angle called \(D\). At vertex P3 there are two angles called \(E\) and \(F\): \begin{align*} C &= \pi - A \\ D &= A - C = 2A - \pi \\ E &= \pi - D \\ F &= A - E = 3A - 2\pi \\ \end{align*}

Sides

Set \(s\) to 1. The value of seventh side t can be found as function of angle A and side s: \begin{align*} s &\equiv 1 \\ u &= \cos A \\ t &= 2(\cos A + \cos D + \cos F) \\ &= 2(\cos A + \cos(2A - \pi) + \cos(3A - 2\pi)) \\ &= 2(\cos A - \cos(2A) + \cos(3A)) \\ &= 2(\cos A - (2\cos^2 A - 1) + 4\cos^3 A - 3\cos A) \\ &= 2(4u^3 - 2u^2 - 2u + 1) \\ \end{align*}

Generators

Define two generators \(r\) = B / A and \(y = \frac{r}{r+1} \): \begin{align*} r &\equiv \frac{B}{A} = \frac{5\pi - 6A}{A} = \frac{y}{1-y}, \quad\quad \text{$ 0 \le r \le \infty $} \\ y &\equiv \frac{r}{r+1} = \frac{B}{A + B} = \frac{5\pi - 6A}{5\pi - 5A} , \quad\quad \text{$ 0 \le y \le 1 $} \\ A &= \frac{5}{6+r}\pi = \frac{1 + y}{6/5 - y}\pi \\ \end{align*}

Rational generators

Define rational generators of class \(c\) (1,2,3...) using integers \(p\): \begin{align*} y_c &\equiv \frac{p}{10c}, \quad\quad \text{where $ 0 \le p \le 10c $} \\ r_c &= \frac{p}{10c - p} \\ A_c/\pi &= \frac{5}{r_c+6} = \frac{10c - p}{12c - p} \\ B_c/\pi &= \frac{5r_c}{r_c+6} = \frac{p}{12c - p} \\ r &= \frac{5A_q - 6A_p}{ A_p}, \quad \text{ where $ \frac{A_p}{A_q} \equiv A $} \end{align*} Class 1 generators are eleven: \begin{align} r_1 = \frac{0}{10}, \frac{1}{9}, \frac{2}{8}, \frac{3}{7}, \frac{4}{6}, \frac{5}{5}, \frac{6}{4}, \frac{7}{3}, \frac{8}{2}, \frac{9}{1}, \frac{10}{0} \end{align} Class 2 generators are only ten (removing values repeated in lower class 1): \begin{align} r_2 = \frac{1}{19}, \frac{3}{17}, \frac{5}{15}, \frac{7}{13}, \frac{9}{11}, \frac{11}{9}, \frac{13}{7}, \frac{15}{5}, \frac{17}{3}, \frac{19}{1} \end{align} and so on...

Three degenerated heptagons in black dots. Three equilateral heptagons in red dots. Three more degenerated heptagons in brown dots.
Plot y from top (\(y=0\)) to bottom (\(y=1\)). Plot t from left (\(t = -3\)) to right (\(t = +3\)). For a particular value of \(t\) solve equation on \(u\) and get \(y\) using: \begin{align*} \arccos (u) &= \frac{A_p}{A_q}\pi \\ y &= \frac{5A_q - 6A_p}{5A_q - 5A_p} \\ \end{align*}

For \(t=0\) we solve \( 8u^3 - 4u^2 - 4u + 2 = 0\) and get \(u_1= \frac{-1}{\sqrt{2}}, u_2 = \frac{1}{2}, u_3 = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \) corresponding to three degenerated heptagons: \begin{align} \arccos(-1/\sqrt{2}) = 3\pi/4 &\therefore y = 2/5 \\ \arccos(1/2) = \pi/3 &\therefore y = 9/10 \\ \arccos(1/\sqrt{2}) = \pi/4 &\therefore y = 14/15 \end{align}

For \( t = 1 \) we solve \( 8u^3 - 4u^2 - 4u + 2 = 1\) and get three equilateral heptagons known as {7}, {7/2}, {7/3}: \begin{align} \arccos(-0.62349) = 5\pi/7 &\therefore y = 5/10 \\ \arccos(+0.22252) = 3\pi/7 &\therefore y = 17/20 \\ \arccos(+0.90097) = \pi/7 &\therefore y = 29/30 \end{align}

For \(t = 2\) we solve \( 8u^3 - 4u^2 - 4u + 2 = 2\) and get \(u_1= -\frac{1}{2}, u_2 = 0, u_3 = 1 \) corresponding to another three degenerated heptagons: \begin{align} \arccos(-1/2) = 2\pi/3 &\therefore y = 3/5 \\ \arccos(0) = \pi/2 &\therefore y = 4/5 \\ \arccos(1) = 0 &\therefore y = 5/5 \end{align}

The three 7-symmetry heptagons

Plot y from top (\(y=0\)) to bottom (\(y=1\)). Plot A from left (\(0\)) to right (\(\frac{5}{6}\pi\)) and B from left (\(0\)) to rigth (\( 5\pi \)).

At \(r = \frac{5}{5}\) A and B curves intersects. All angles are equal and represents the regular heptagon {7}.

At \(r = \frac{17}{3}\) \(B - A = 2\pi \). This is the case of heptagon {7/2}. It seems \(B\) equals \(A\) but is not, exceeds \(A \) by a complete turn, so {7/2} is equilateral but not regular.

At \(r = \frac{29}{1}\) \(B - A = 4\pi \). This is case of heptagon {7/3}. It seems \(B\) equals \(A\) but is not, \(B\) exceeds \(A\) by two complete turns so {7/3} is equilateral but not regular.
