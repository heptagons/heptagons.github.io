(As)2B(sA)2t

(As)2B(sA)2t is the class of pentagons with
  1. Four equal angles A and a single angle B.
  2. Four equal sides s and a single side t.

(As)2B(sA)2t pentagon

Angles A,B

For such an pentagon we have (\( 0 \le A \le 3\pi/4 \)) and (\( 0 \le B \le 3\pi\)): \begin{align*} 4A + B &= 3\pi \end{align*} At vertex P1 there is an angle called \( C \). At vertex P2 there is an angle called \(D\). \begin{align*} C &= \pi - A \\ D &= A - C \\ &= 2A - \pi \end{align*}

Sides

Set \(e_{1...4}\) size s to 1. The value of seventh side t can be found as function of angle A and side s: \begin{align*} s &\equiv 1 \\ u &= \cos A \\ t &= 2(\cos A + \cos D) \\ &= 2(\cos A + \cos(2A - \pi)) \\ &= 2(\cos A - \cos(2A))) \\ &= 2(\cos A - (2\cos^2 A - 1)) \\ &= 2(-2u^2 + u + 1) \\ \end{align*}

Generators

Define two generators \(r\) = B / A and \(y = \frac{r}{r+1} \): \begin{align*} r &\equiv \frac{B}{A} = \frac{3\pi - 4A}{A} = \frac{y}{1-y}, \quad\quad \text{$ 0 \le r \le \infty $} \\ y &\equiv \frac{r}{r+1} = \frac{B}{A + B} = \frac{3\pi - 4A}{3\pi - 3A} , \quad\quad \text{$ 0 \le y \le 1 $} \\ A &= \frac{3}{4+r}\pi = \frac{1 + y}{4/3 - y}\pi \\ \end{align*}

Rational generators

Define rational generators of class \(c\) (1,2,3...) using integers \(p\): \begin{align*} y_c &\equiv \frac{p}{6c}, \quad\quad \text{where $ 0 \le p \le 6c $} \\ r_c &= \frac{p}{6c - p} \\ A_c/\pi &= \frac{3}{r_c+4} = \frac{10c - p}{8c - p} \\ B_c/\pi &= \frac{3r_c}{r_c+4} = \frac{p}{8c - p} \\ r &= \frac{3A_q - 4A_p}{ A_p}, \quad \text{ where $ \frac{A_p}{A_q} \equiv A $} \end{align*} Class 1 generators are seven: \begin{align} r_1 = \frac{0}{6}, \frac{1}{5}, \frac{2}{4}, \frac{3}{3}, \frac{4}{2}, \frac{5}{1}, \frac{6}{0} \end{align} Class 2 generators are only six (removing values repeated in lower class 1): \begin{align} r_2 = \frac{1}{11}, \frac{3}{9}, \frac{5}{7}, \frac{7}{5}, \frac{9}{3}, \frac{11}{1} \end{align} and so on...

Two degenerated pentagons in black dots. Two equilateral pentagons in red dots. Two more degenerated pentagons in brown dots.
Plot y from top (\(y=0\)) to bottom (\(y=1\)). Plot t from left (\(t = -3\)) to right (\(t = +3\)). For a particular value of \(t\) solve equation on \(u\) and get \(y\) using: \begin{align*} \arccos (u) &= \frac{A_p}{A_q}\pi \\ y &= \frac{3A_q - 4A_p}{3A_q - 3A_p} \\ \end{align*}

For \(t=0\) we solve \( -4u^2 + 2u + 2 = 0 \) and get \(u_1 = -\frac{1}{2}, u_2 = 1 \) corresponding to two degenerated pentagons: \begin{align} \arccos(-1/2) = 2\pi/3 &\therefore y = 1/3 \\ \arccos(1) = 0 &\therefore y = 3/3 \end{align}

For \( t = 1 \) we solve \( -4u^2 + 2u + 2 = 1 \) and get \(u_1 = \frac{1-\sqrt{5}}{4}, u_2 = \frac{1+\sqrt{5}}{4} \) corresponding to two equilateral pentagons known as {5}, {5/2}: \begin{align} \arccos((1-\sqrt{5})/4) = 3\pi/5 &\therefore y = 3/6 \\ \arccos((1+\sqrt{5})/4) = \pi/5 &\therefore y = 11/12 \end{align}

For \(t = 2\) we solve \( -4u^2 + 2u + 2 = 2 \) and get \(u_1 = 0, u_2 = \frac{1}{2} \) corresponding to another two degenerated pentagons: \begin{align} \arccos(0) = \pi/2 &\therefore y = 2/3 \\ \arccos(1/2) = \pi/3 &\therefore y= 5/6 \end{align}

The two 5-symmetry heptagons

Plot y from top (\(y=0\)) to bottom (\(y=1\)). Plot A from left (\(0\)) to right (\(\frac{5}{6}\pi\)) and B from left (\(0\)) to rigth (\( 5\pi \)).

At \(r = \frac{5}{5}\) A and B curves intersects. All angles are equal and represents the regular heptagon {7}.

At \(r = \frac{17}{3}\) \(B - A = 2\pi \). This is the case of heptagon {7/2}. It seems \(B\) equals \(A\) but is not, exceeds \(A \) by a complete turn, so {7/2} is equilateral but not regular.

At \(r = \frac{29}{1}\) \(B - A = 4\pi \). This is case of heptagon {7/3}. It seems \(B\) equals \(A\) but is not, \(B\) exceeds \(A\) by two complete turns so {7/3} is equilateral but not regular.
