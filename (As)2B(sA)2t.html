(As)2B(sA)2t

The (As)2B(sA)2t polygon is the pentagon with four equal angles A and a single angle B. Also has four equal sides s and a single side t.

(As)2B(sA)2t pentagon

Angles A,B

For such an pentagon we have (\( 0 \le A \le 3\pi/4 \)) and (\( 0 \le B \le 3\pi\)): \begin{align*} 4A + B &= 3\pi \end{align*} At vertex P1 there is an angle called \( C \). At vertex P2 there is an angle called \(D\). \begin{align*} C &= \pi - A \\ D &= A - C \\ &= 2A - \pi \end{align*}

Sides

Set \(s_{1...6}\) as 1. The value of seventh side t can be found as function of angle A and side s: \begin{align*} s_{1...4} &\equiv 1 \\ u &= \cos A \\ t &= 2(\cos A + \cos D) \\ &= 2(\cos A + \cos(2A - \pi)) \\ &= 2(\cos A - \cos(2A))) \\ &= 2(\cos A - (2\cos^2 A - 1)) \\ &= 2(-2u^2 + u + 1) \\ \end{align*}

Generators

Define two generators \(r\) = B / A and \(y = \frac{r}{r+1} \): \begin{align*} r &\equiv \frac{B}{A} = \frac{3\pi - 4A}{A} = \frac{y}{1-y}, \quad\quad \text{$ 0 \le r \le \infty $} \\ y &\equiv \frac{r}{r+1} = \frac{B}{A + B} = \frac{3\pi - 4A}{3\pi - 3A} , \quad\quad \text{$ 0 \le y \le 1 $} \\ A &= \frac{3}{4+r}\pi = \frac{1 + y}{4/3 - y}\pi \\ \end{align*}

Rational generators

Define rational generators of class \(c\) (1,2,3...) using integers \(p\): \begin{align*} y_c &\equiv \frac{p}{6c}, \quad\quad \text{where $ 0 \le p \le 6c $} \\ r_c &= \frac{p}{6c - p} \\ A_c/\pi &= \frac{3}{r_c+4} = \frac{10c - p}{8c - p} \\ B_c/\pi &= \frac{3r_c}{r_c+4} = \frac{p}{8c - p} \\ r &= \frac{3A_q - 4A_p}{ A_p}, \quad \text{ where $ \frac{A_p}{A_q} \equiv A $} \end{align*} Class 1 generators are seven: \begin{align} r_1 = \frac{0}{6}, \frac{1}{5}, \frac{2}{4}, \frac{3}{3}, \frac{4}{2}, \frac{5}{1}, \frac{6}{0} \end{align} Class 2 generators are only six (removing values repeated in lower class 1): \begin{align} r_2 = \frac{1}{11}, \frac{3}{9}, \frac{5}{7}, \frac{7}{5}, \frac{9}{3}, \frac{11}{1} \end{align} and so on...

Side graph

Two degenerated pentagons in black dots. Two equilateral pentagons in red dots. Two more degenerated pentagons in brown dots.
Plot y from top (\(y=0\)) to bottom (\(y=1\)). Plot t from left (\(t = -3\)) to right (\(t = +3\)) with: \begin{align*} u &= \cos(\frac{1 + y}{4/3 - y}\pi) \\ \end{align*}

For \(t=0\) we solve \( -4u^2 + 2u + 2 = 0 \) and get \(u= -\frac{1}{2}, 1 \) corresponding to two degenerated pentagons: \begin{align} \arccos(-1/2) &= 2\pi/3 = A|_{y=x/x} \\ \arccos(1) &= 0 = A|_{y=6/6} \\ \end{align}

For \( t = 1 \) we solve \( -4u^2 + 2u + 2 = 1 \) and get \(u= \frac{1-\sqrt{5}}{4}, \frac{1+\sqrt{5}}{4} \) corresponding to two equilateral pentagons known as {5}, {5/2}: \begin{align} \arccos((1-\sqrt{5})/4) &= n\pi/d = A|_{y=x/x} \\ \arccos((1+\sqrt{5})/4) &= n\pi/d = A|_{y=x/x} \\ \end{align}

For \(t = 2\) we solve \( -4u^2 + 2u + 2 = 2 \) and get another two degenerated pentagons: \begin{align} \arccos(0) &= \pi/2 = A|_{y=x/x} \\ \arccos(1/2) &= \pi/3 = A|_{y=x/x} \\ \end{align}
